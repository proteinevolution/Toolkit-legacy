CASP and CAFASP

The Critical Assessment of Structure Prediction or CASP is a blind, biannual protein structure prediction benchmark in which both human predictors and automatized servers participate. The recent benchmark, CASP7, ran from May to August 2006. The targets used for the benchmark are protein structures that are obtained by the CASP organizers directly from the experimentalists, mainly structural genomics labs, before being released.

The Critical Assessment of Fully Automated Structure Prediction CAFASP5 is held in parallel to CASP and uses the models submitted to CASP. It is geared more specifically towards comparison of automatic structure prediction servers. It differs from CASP in employing solely automated assessment procedures without expert inspection of models.

MaxSub and MaxSubDom scores

In CAFASP, the quality of a predicted 3D structural model is assessed by the MaxSub score and the derived MaxSubDom score. Simply speaking, the MaxSub score tells us what fraction of the model residues can be structurally superposed with the corresponding residues from the target structure. The numbers in the result tables above are MaxSub scores multiplied by 100. A score of 63 indicates that ~63% of the model residues are approximately correctly predicted. [Siew N, Elofsson A, Rychlewski L, Fischer D. (2000) Bioinformatics 16:776]

MaxSubDom is useful for assessing each domain of a multi-domain model separately, independent of their relative orientation. "It is an iterative application of MaxSub to find different rotations and translations that may still have significant matches. We first apply MaxSub as usual and then ignore all the residues that were found to be well-predicted. Then we re-apply MaxSub (without the well-predicted residues identified in the previous step) to see if there is a large enough second subset and if so, the score is added to the previous score." (Prof. Dan Fischer)
